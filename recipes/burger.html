Burger Recipe

Description

There is nothing quite like a homemade classic beef burger fresh off the grill or pan. This recipe delivers a perfectly juicy patty with a beautifully seared crust, seasoned simply to let the quality of the beef shine through. Topped with melted cheddar, crisp lettuce, ripe tomato, and your favourite condiments, it is the ultimate comfort food that never goes out of style.

Whether you are hosting a backyard barbecue or just craving a satisfying weeknight dinner, this burger comes together in under 30 minutes and can be easily customised with bacon, avocado, onion rings, or any toppings you love.

Ingredients

  • 500 g (1 lb) ground beef (80/20 blend)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese
  • 4 burger buns, split and toasted
  • 4 leaves iceberg lettuce
  • 1 large tomato, sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • Ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise to taste
  • Pickles (optional)

Steps

  1. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions and shape each into a patty about 1 cm (½ inch) thicker than your bun. Press a small indent in the centre of each patty to prevent it from puffing up during cooking.
  2. Season both sides generously with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Let the patties rest at room temperature for 10 minutes.
  3. Heat a grill, griddle, or heavy skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the patties for 4 minutes on the first side without moving them.
  4. Flip the patties and place a slice of cheese on each. Cook for another 3-4 minutes for medium doneness, or adjust to your preference.
  5. While the patties finish cooking, toast the burger buns cut-side down on the grill or in a separate pan until golden.
  6. Assemble the burgers: spread condiments on the bottom bun, add lettuce and tomato, place the patty with melted cheese on top, then add onion slices and pickles. Cap with the top bun and serve immediately.
